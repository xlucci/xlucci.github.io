The Great Beef Experiment

Ribeye steak of two kinds, USDA prime and USDA choice, was separated into two piles each and liberally seasoned with salt and pepper. The four different plates of beef were then either grilled or sous vide'd and served to members of the mitBEEF community, who then each rated the four different slices in descending order by taste preference. From this it was concluded that the average member of the mitBEEF community could not distinguish between grilled prime and choice steak, but that due to high variability in serving temperature and presentation the data should be interpreted as inconclusive.
